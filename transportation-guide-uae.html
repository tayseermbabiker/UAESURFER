UAE Transportation Guide

Metro, taxis, Uber, car rentals & more

How do you get around Dubai?

Dubai has an excellent modern transport network. The Dubai Metro is the fastest and cheapest way to cover long distances, with two lines serving major attractions. Taxis are affordable and metered starting from AED 12. Ride-hailing apps Careem and Uber work throughout the UAE. Buses connect areas the metro does not reach, and water taxis cross Dubai Creek.

How to Get Around

Dubai Metro: Best value AED 3-7 per trip. Clean, fast, covers major attractions. Buy a Nol card.
Uber & Careem: Convenient Both work great. AED 25-60 typical trip. Careem accepts cash.
Car rental: From AED 80/day Easy to drive. International license accepted. Excellent roads.
Best combo: Metro for major attractions + Uber/taxi for specific destinations. Budget AED 50-100/day.

The Basics

  • Red Line: Airport → Downtown → Marina → JBR (most tourist areas)
  • Green Line: Old Dubai, Deira, Creek, Festival City
  • Hours: 5am-midnight (1am Fridays)
  • Frequency: Every 3-4 minutes (peak), 7-10 minutes (off-peak)

Nol Card (Required)

  • Buy at any metro station (AED 25 with AED 19 credit)
  • Tap in at entry gate, tap out at exit
  • Also works on buses and trams
  • Refundable AED 6 deposit when leaving UAE

Fares

  • 1 zone: AED 3
  • 2 zones: AED 5
  • 3+ zones: AED 7.50
  • Day pass: AED 20 unlimited
  • Gold Class: 2x regular fare (less crowded)

Key Stations for Tourists

  • Burj Khalifa/Dubai Mall: Downtown Dubai
  • DMCC/Dubai Marina: Marina, JBR Beach
  • Mall of Emirates: Ski Dubai
  • Al Ras: Gold Souk, Old Dubai
Rush hour tip: 7-9am and 5-8pm are crowded. Gold Class is worth it during peak times.
Local Tip: Buy a Nol Silver card at any metro station for AED 25 (AED 19 credit). It works on metro, buses, trams, and water buses. Much cheaper than buying single tickets every time.

Ride Apps

  • Uber: Works great, similar to taxi prices
  • Careem: Local favorite, often slightly cheaper, accepts cash
  • Hala: Official RTA app, no surge pricing
  • Tip: Download both Uber & Careem, compare prices

Official Taxis (Beige Color)

  • Base fare: AED 5 (AED 25 from airport)
  • Per km: AED 1.96
  • Minimum fare: AED 12
  • All metered, safe, can pay by card

Typical Costs

  • Airport → Downtown: AED 50-70
  • Marina → Downtown: AED 40-55
  • Short trips: AED 15-30
  • Dubai → Abu Dhabi: AED 250-300
Surge pricing: Uber surges during rush hour/rain. Hala app (official taxis) has no surge — good backup option.
Local Tip: Avoid the metro between 7-9 AM and 5-7 PM on weekdays. Trains get extremely crowded during rush hour. The Gold Class carriage at the front is worth the extra cost during peak times.

Requirements

  • Age: 21+ (25+ for luxury cars)
  • License: International Driving Permit + home license (US, UK, EU licenses often accepted directly)
  • Credit card: For deposit (AED 1,500-5,000 hold)

Costs

  • Economy: AED 80-120/day
  • Mid-size: AED 120-180/day
  • SUV: AED 200-350/day
  • Fuel: Cheap (~AED 2.5-3/liter)

Driving in UAE

  • Drive on the right (like US/Europe)
  • Speed limits: 60-80 km/h city, 100-140 km/h highway
  • Automatic speed cameras everywhere
  • Salik toll gates: AED 4-5 per crossing
  • Parking: Free at malls, paid in city (AED 2-4/hour)
Speeding fines: Cameras catch everything. 20 km/h over = AED 300. Fines deducted from rental deposit.

Dubai Airport (DXB)

  • Metro: AED 5-7, connects Terminals 1 & 3. Best value!
  • Taxi: AED 50-90 depending on destination
  • Uber/Careem: Similar to taxi, pickup at designated areas
  • Hotel shuttle: Many 4-5 star hotels offer free pickup

Abu Dhabi Airport (AUH)

  • Bus A1: AED 4 to downtown Abu Dhabi
  • Taxi: AED 60-100 within Abu Dhabi
  • To Dubai: AED 300-350 by taxi
Late arrival tip: If landing after midnight when metro is closed, pre-book a transfer (AED 100-150) or use Uber/taxi.

Dubai ↔ Abu Dhabi

  • Distance: 150 km, 1.5-2 hours
  • Bus: E100/E101 from Al Ghubaiba, AED 25, every 20-40 mins
  • Taxi: AED 250-300 one-way
  • Rental car: Easy highway drive

Dubai ↔ Sharjah

  • Distance: 30 km, 30-60 mins (traffic dependent)
  • Bus: AED 10, every 15-20 mins
  • Warning: Rush hour border traffic is brutal

Dubai ↔ Fujairah (East Coast)

  • Distance: 130 km, 1.5-2 hours
  • Bus: E700, AED 25
  • Scenic mountain drive if renting car
Multiple day trips? Rent a car for flexibility. AED 100/day + fuel beats multiple taxi fares.

Do's

  • Use the Dubai Metro for speed and savings on major routes
  • Download the Careem and Uber apps before arriving
  • Buy a Nol card for all public transport
  • Agree on taxi fare before long trips to other emirates

Don'ts

  • Eat or drink on the Dubai Metro as fines are AED 200
  • Take unmetered taxis especially near tourist spots
  • Drive during Friday prayer times when roads empty then suddenly flood
  • Rent a car unless you are comfortable with aggressive driving
